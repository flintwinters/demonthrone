- Project state: playable browser proof of concept for a crisp Three.js/WebGL isometric squad-tactics roguelike on a coordinate-unbounded procedural voxel grid.
- Root `manage.py` is the centralized project entrypoint: bare `python manage.py` (also exposed as `npm start`) builds the frontend and starts the backend, `python manage.py build` only compiles, and the explicit `python manage.py runserver --no-build` escape hatch serves existing output.
- TypeScript source lives in `ts/`; compiled browser modules are emitted recursively to ignored `src/**/*.js` output by `npm run build` and must not be committed. The handwritten `src/styles.css` remains tracked. Cold subsystems live in `controls/`, `world/`, `visibility/`, and `rendering/`; imports crossing those directory boundaries must use the module `index.ts` facade.
- Runtime entity collections, selection, and hovered/selected tiles are assembled into the explicit `GameState` aggregate in `ts/game-state.ts`; board, visibility, inspection, and action-field selectors receive that state instead of mixing injected arrays with hidden singleton reads.
- Reusable object-oriented domain classes for biome profiles, terrain kinds, safe zones, noise layers, height profiles, and typed entity templates live in `ts/domain.ts`; feature modules should compose those classes rather than rebuilding parallel tables.
- `ts/game-config.ts` owns exactly three typed elevation Perlin layers spanning macro, terrain-shaping, and detail wavelengths; every layer independently configures wavelength in tiles, output amplitude, seed, and sample transformation, and every biome composes those layers with its own base elevation.
- Elevation samples divide world coordinates by wavelength, apply the selected transformation to normalized Perlin noise, then multiply by amplitude. Integer terrain mapping is intentionally unclamped.
- Terrain height mapping uses `mappingMin`/`mappingMax` as its nominal output scale while wider `min`/`max` bounds cover unclamped rendering and camera picking.
- Each biome owns typed boulder, foliage, water, and ice feature configuration in `ts/biome-config.ts`, including independent Perlin parameters, thresholds, and foliage sight cost.
- Entity contracts form a discriminated hierarchy in `ts/types.ts`: all entities have stable identity and archetype metadata, damageable characters and world objects share health, and teammate/enemy/pushable branches add only their own state. Add new archetypes through the matching template subclass rather than constructing ad hoc object literals.
- Every terrain and entity contract requires concise user-facing `infoText`; terrain types and entity archetype configuration own the value, templates propagate it to runtime instances, and selection status reads it directly instead of deriving labels from internal kind/type identifiers.
- Enemy archetypes are configured in `ts/character-config.ts` with typed combat stats, movement cadence, and appearance dimensions shared by rendering and 3D sight blockers. Pursuers are range-2 cones; nephilim are tall cylinders with 8 health, 3 damage, and movement every third enemy phase.
- Generic tile identity, entity-at-tile lookup, cardinal adjacency, and Manhattan distance helpers live in `ts/grid.ts`; feature modules should import them instead of redefining tile math or coordinate searches.
- The deterministic world currently classifies cinder, fen, heath, ridge, bog, and mesa biomes and supports coordinate-unbounded height-aware Three.js voxel rendering with high-contrast integer terrain and height-tinted vertical side-face gradients.
- The player roster contains vanguard, warden, varmint, and marauder teammates with independently configured sight, movement, attack range, and health. Current enemy archetypes are procedurally materialized pursuers and nephilim.
- Current terrain and prop scope includes sparse biome-weighted boulders, passable biome-colored high-LOS-cost crossed-triangle foliage with stable coordinate-varied heights, passable basin-filled Perlin water with movement cost 10 and sight cost 0.1, and passable basin-filled Perlin ice with movement cost 0.5 and sight cost 0.1.
- Current interaction scope includes panning, positive wheel/pinch zooming, right-drag pitch capped at the straight-down bird's-eye pole, horizontal rotation, on-screen rotation controls, exact height-field mouse picking across top and side faces, hover and tile selection, compact overhead health labels, teammate attacks against enemies and crates with entity-only range highlights and attacker-specific elevation-aware line of sight, living teammate/enemy sight blockers, coordinate-unbounded enemy/crate materialization with consumed-origin retention, enemy pursuit before player planning, line-of-sight enemy attacks during turn resolution without a same-phase move, one-turn gray sphere tombstones, shadowless teammate markers, enemy inspection fields, teammate-pushable crates with simultaneous previews, crate-first enchantment chains and downstream dispelling, unified click precedence and click-again/teammate/Escape cancellation, and team-visibility-connected obstacle-aware simultaneous planned movement with reciprocal exponential slope costs and transparent destination previews.
- Deterministic coordinate-unbounded rivers and multi-tile-thick walls follow narrow contours of independent low-frequency Perlin fields. Wall contours occupy the relatively rare elevated noise band from 0.65 through 0.70, whose modestly increased width produces better-supported walkable tops without making walls common. Rivers take crossing precedence and reuse passable water terrain with movement cost 10 and sight cost 0.1. A smooth envelope divides walls into finite segments that taper into climbable ends. Wall elevation combines a configured proportion of natural terrain with a twelve-level tapered masonry target, subtracts a second independent Perlin layer, floors the terrain-relative rise at zero so ordinary ground can absorb the wall, and halves only the remaining positive rise. This keeps the shorter fieldstone walls responsive to the terrain curve. Walls remain ordinary prop-free biome blocks so existing slope rules alone govern access to their tops.
- Wall terrain uses a neutral-gray material independent of biome with stable coordinate-varied per-block brightness and a procedurally generated, nearest-filtered, world-aligned repeating brick texture on both top and side faces. Dark-gray mortar lines contrast with lighter varied brick faces without becoming black, and the masonry pattern remains in the terrain face batches.
- Enemy attacks require both attack range and exact 3D line of sight through terrain, boulders, and living character blockers; walls therefore prevent unseen attacks using the same occlusion geometry as player visibility.
- Performance state: terrain faces are batched in `ts/rendering/terrain-batch.ts`, boulder/brush props render through `InstancedMesh` in `ts/rendering/terrain-props.ts`, and renderer visibility results are cached across camera/hover redraws in `ts/rendering/renderer.ts`.
- Visibility fields use cached octant shadowcasting with three angular samples per former bin and per-tile nearest-geometric-center-or-majority footprint aggregation, while preserving slope-scaled weighted range cost, threefold enemy-occupancy sight cost, and vertical terrain/boulder/character horizons in quadratic work; attacker-specific entity LOS retains the exact native 3D supercover ray traversal.
- Movement and attack overlays are cached per-unit fields: teammate movement uses one obstacle-aware weighted flood fill constrained to contiguous shared team-visible terrain, enemy inspection movement remains visibility-unrestricted, and attacks reuse the 3D shadowcasting geometry with attack range as the budget and unit traversal cost.
- Inspecting an enemy with no teammate selected displays that enemy's cached movement and attack fields without making the enemy actionable.
- A compact upper-right cardinal compass rotates with camera azimuth while keeping its direction labels upright.
- Player planning has one shared teammate action per turn total. The first planned movement, push, attack, or enchantment reserves the team action until that plan is explicitly cancelled or the next planning phase resets it.
- Visibility recomputation memoizes procedural tile properties across all teammates, supersamples each outer-radius angular bin threefold, samples shared tile properties once per depth footprint, skips out-of-radius samples before world lookup, and relies on a bounded 32,768-tile world-data cache sized to retain several overlapping sight fields during movement.
- When the final teammate dies, fog of war is bypassed in a configurable 20-tile Euclidean radius centered on the final death position and a compact red `game over` status appears at the top of the screen.
- Python backend lives in `backend/`, uses only the standard library, tolerates clients disconnecting during static responses, serves the static frontend, and exposes `/api/health` plus `/api/game/new`.
- Verification gate: run `npm run check`; ESLint enforces cyclomatic complexity and file length limits.
- Backend verification gate: run `python -m unittest discover`.
- Public-repository hygiene: generated Python bytecode, local environment files, private-key formats, coverage output, and logs are ignored.
- The backend serves only the runtime allowlist (`index.html`, compiled `src/`, the Three.js browser build, and `/favicon.ico` plus `/favicon.png` aliases to `public/favicon.png`); repository metadata, TypeScript, backend source, tests, and traversal paths must remain inaccessible over HTTP.
- UI direction: dense high-contrast gruvbox dark theme with bright, moderately saturated biome families and interaction colors, minimal labels, no animations or transitions; foliage is deliberately dimmer than focus pieces, remains visibly colored and green-biased, and uses explicit geometry normals to participate in strong scene lighting rather than glowing unlit or rendering black.
- The lower-left selection status contains concise context-sensitive interaction guidance or selected entity/terrain info, with no heading or supplemental statistics; internal brush terrain is labeled `foliage`.
- Use TypeScript as much as possible. Try not to hand-edit JavaScript.
- Stop rm-ing __pycache__
